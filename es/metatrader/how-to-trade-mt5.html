How to trade Forex

A Forex trader has one objective in mind when trading which is:

To exchange one currency for another in order to make profit.

But if you are new to Forex or financial trading of any kind, there is still a long way to go before you even purchase your first contract. This is why we have come up with a basic, step-by-step tutorial to help you bridge that gap and make your first trade.

Learn to read currency pairs

Step 1

Can't make heads or tails of currency pairs? Here is a simple way of looking at it:
EUR

Based currency

Also known as transaction currency
USD

Quote currency

Also known as counter money

Tips:

  • The base currency is always equal to one unit
  • The price of the currency pair indicates how much of the quote currency is required to buy one unit of base currency. This is more commonly known as the exchange rate.

Example:

If you see EUR/USD has a bid price of 1.05229, you will sell USD 1.05229 for every EUR 1 that you buy.

Understand when to buy and sell

Step 2

You want to buy a certain currency pair if you think the base currency will go up. The reverse holds true:

You want to sell that currency pair if you think the base currency will go down.

You have probably seen the terms 'going long' and 'going short' being used by a lot of Forex brokers and traders. Let's compare the differences between the two terms:

Long:

  • Go long = buy
  • You are buying the base currency and selling the quote currency.
  • You are expecting the base currency to rise in value so you can sell it back for a profit.

Short:

  • Go short = sell
  • You are selling the base currency and buying the quote currency.
  • You are expecting the base currency to fall in value so you can buy it back at a lower price and make profit.

Example:

After your analysis, you have come to the conclusion that the British economy will outperform its U.S. counterpart. Thus, you decide to go long and buy into the GBP/USD, expecting the GBP to rise in value.

If you decided that the British economy will underperform instead, then you will go short on the GBP/USD.

Buy your first currency pairs

Step 3

After you have decided which position you want to take whether long or short you are well on your way to buying your first currency pair.

Example:

GBP/USD currency pair and its bid-ask price.

To go long, you will click on 'Buy' to purchase GBP 1 for USD 1.22781. To go short, you will click on 'Sell' to sell GBP 1 and receive USD 1.22771 in return.

How to trade Metals?

Learn the basic concepts of trading Meta assets on MetaTrader 5.

When you are trading metals, you can choose to open a buy position if you think that the price of an asset will rise or a sell position if you think that the price of an asset will fall.

When to buy and sell?

Can't figure it out on when to buy and sell? Here is a simple way of looking at it:

ComprarIn this case, you predict that the price will rise. This is also known as 'going long'.
VentaIn this case, you predict that the price will fall. This is also known as 'going short'.

Example: XAU/USD (Gold vs US Dollar) symbol pair

If you decide to buy or 'go long' on the XAU/USD, you will profit if the price of the XAU/USD rises, and incur losses if the price falls.

If you decide to sell or 'go short' on XAU/USD, you will profit if the price of the XAU/USD falls, and incur losses if the price rises.

Factors that affect precious metal prices

Knowing when to buy and sell precious metals largely depends on how well you know the factors that influence market prices. The most common influencing factors are:

  • Supply - Decline or increase in supply
  • Demand – Driven by industrial and commercial applications, including new technological products or fashion trends
  • Market volatility – Political, economic, or social instability may lead to more volatile financial markets that affect the prices of certain metals

As conclusion, keeping up with market news and trends in a certain sector can help you always
